Do you lose the ability to pucker or kiss after Botox lip flip?
Botox lip flip
Jan 26, 2021
Thank you for your question. Botox lip flip can affect your ability to pucker your lips, drink out of a straw or kiss. Since Botox is a neurotoxin it relaxes the muscles which can affect your ability to move your mouth. Since the dosing for the lip flip is very small, usually patients only notice the difference in movement when trying to purse their lips more intensely. Botox typically begins to wear off at the 3-month mark.
My Latisse expired in 2016. Is it OK to use?
Latisse use
Jan 26, 2021
Thank you for your question. No, you should never use a product or prescription past its expiration date. Please see a board-certified dermatologist to get a prescription that is within use date.
Does Latisse regrow eyebrows?
Latisse for eyebrows
Jan 26, 2021
Thank you for your question. The use of Latisse on the eyebrows is off-label, but has been done. Yes, Latisse may help your eyebrow hairs regrow. It will help to thicken follicles that are still there, but does not create new ones. Please go to a board-certified dermatologist to see if Latisse would be a good fit for you.

Laser Genesis

How long after the first Emsculpt treatment do you see results?
EmSculpt results
Jan 12, 2021
Thank you for your question. It does typically take 1-3 months to see maximal results after EmSculpt. However, many people do begin to feel their muscles in the treated area getting stronger even after just a few treatments. Results are best maintained by living a healthy lifestyle, such as eating healthy and exercising. It is also beneficial to have EmSculpt maintenance treatments to maintain those results from your series of EmSculpt treatments. Maintenance can be a once a month treatment.

Jun 19, 2020

When can one have Emsculpt after giving birth?
Emsculpt after pregnancy
Apr 16, 2020
Thank you for your question and congratulations on your new baby! Along with improving muscle tone and burning fat, Emsculpt can also help treat “diastasis recti” also known as the separation of the abdominal muscles. This is common during pregnancy. It’s best to wait at least 3-6 months post partum and no longer breastfeeding to begin any treatment. Please seek a board-certified dermatologist to determine if you are an ideal candidate for this treatment at that time.
