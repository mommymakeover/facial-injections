Best Time To Do Botox Is When You Need It & Are Ready

I have been injecting Botox for cosmetic reasons since 1991. I have not personally found, nor is there any data to suggest that Botox works any better when injected during one season or another. What I can say is that repeated use of dynamic wrinkles (wrinkles related to the motion of the muscles of facial expression) eventually leads to the development of etched-in static wrinkle lines (wrinkles at rest).

The Time Of Year To Do Botox Is Irrelevant

When is the best time to do Botox?

Botox acts to paralyze the muscles of facial expression through a series of tiny injections. Because it acts on the muscles below the skin, Botox does not affect sun sensitivity like some types of facial rejuvenation procedures can (laser treatments, chemical peels, tretinoin cream).

You can feel comfortable having Botox done any time of the year. During your appointment, your physician will carefully plan where to inject the Botox in order to improve lines on your forehead, glabellar region (between your eyebrows) or crows feet, depending on your individual anatomy and goals.

Botox generally starts to take effect around 3-4 days after it is injected, and about 7 days before the full effect is noticed. It then lasts around 4 months. For this reason, patients will often have injections several times a year. (Claire S. Duggal, MD, Annapolis Plastic Surgeon)

You may have Botox winter,spring,summer or fall. (Brian K. Reedy, MD, Reading Plastic Surgeon)

Best time for Botox is now and every 3-4 months

Botox is a great treatment to relax the muscles around your eyes that you use to squint and frown. We use it in many other places in the face and neck as well. There is no recommended season for using it.

It is best to repeat it every 3-4 months so that the muscles never fully return to full function. When Botox is used in this way, the wrinkles that you already started to get soften and start to recover and other wrinkles are prevented from forming. (Quenby Erickson, DO, FAAD, FACMS, Chicago Dermatologic Surgeon)
