How does it work?

Botox is a high activity drug that contains weakened botulinum neurotoxins (natural proteins). Their introduction into the skin in small doses makes it possible to relieve muscle tension in such a way that after relaxation, wrinkles are smoothed, the skin is leveled and the wrinkles are eliminated.

Injections are carried out with particularly thin needles, so that they can be injected directly into the muscles and block their activity, i.e. relax.

Probably, you noticed that wrinkles appear from bright emotions or face tension, and independently muscles can not stop straining. Botox “frees” the musculature of the face from any work, thereby updating the texture and condition of the skin, all grooves and irregularities are leveled. For treatment (eg, hyperhidrosis), this procedure is allowed for patients who reached the age of 9, while contraindications should be considered.

Contraindications

  • Pregnancy

The technique of injecting varies depending on the goal pursued, but there are common points. Immediately before the injection, a local anesthetic is performed (usually a cream or spray), since unpleasant pain from the injections is possible.
