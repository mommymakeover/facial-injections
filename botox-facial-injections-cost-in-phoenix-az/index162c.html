Botulinum toxin is a strong poison, when ingested (when eating contaminated foods) it paralyzes the nervous system and motor activity of the muscles.

But four hundred years ago the German physician Paracelsus wrote: “Everything is poison, nothing is poisonous, only a dose makes poison medicine.” These words are easily confirmed by examples:

Aspirin, acetylsalicylic acid: 0.5 grams – medicine, 30 grams – lethal dose.
Food salt, sodium chloride: 2-3 grams is a daily human need for table salt, a single use of 200-300 grams is a lethal dose.
Botox (its quantity is measured not in grams, but in unit of action): 30 units is the average dose used to correct wrinkles, 3000 units is a lethal dose.

Thus, the relative toxicity of Botox is comparable to the toxicity of table salt: exceeding the dosage by 100 times is deadly. “Only a dose makes the medicine a medicine.”

Safe poison!

The safety of Botox is confirmed by more than 200 clinical trials conducted in independent clinics of different countries. It is proved that in therapeutic doses the preparation has only a local effect, without exerting any influence on the organism as a whole.

That local action is completely reversible: even multiple (more than 30) injections of Botox into one and the same place do not cause damage to either nerves or muscles. Only temporarily, for several months, the transmission of a nerve impulse to the muscle is blocked.

Currently, drugs based on botulinum toxin, Botox and Dysport, is widely used not only in cosmetology, but also in such areas of medicine as neurology, pediatrics.
