How Long After Botox Can You See Results?

Botox Results Following Treatment

It typically takes anywhere from 5-7 days for Botox to begin working completely. Some patients will notice results even sooner, but most patients experience the full effect closer to a week following injections.

There is no exact limit as to how frequently injections can be performed. As far as how long it can be repeated, many patients have been treated regularly for many years.

In fact, several patients of my practice who have been undergoing injections for several years require less frequent and lower-dose injections because of progressive smoothing from continued regular Botox injections. (Jeffrey Ridha, MD, Saratoga Springs Plastic Surgeon)

Give BOTOX Time to Develop

I’ve heard some of my BOTOX patients say they start to see some improvement after only a couple of days, but it takes a bit longer for most people. It’s generally accepted that it takes about 7 to 10 days before full results are apparent.

At this point, you should see a significant improvement in the wrinkles that were treated. Repeated injections of BOTOX spaced several months apart have not been shown to be harmful, and there’s no reason that you can’t undergo injections indefinitely.

Although more research is needed, anecdotal evidence suggests that people who regularly undergo BOTOX treatments often enjoy longer-lasting results, eventually spacing their appointments more than 4 months apart. (Howard Silverman, MD, FRCSC, Ottawa Plastic Surgeon)

Botox Onset and How Long it Lasts.

Botox takes about 2 days to start seeing the effect with 5 days until the full effect. This may vary with areas of injection and amount of injection (number of units). Botox does last about 3 to 4 months.

Repetitive treatment can cause muscle atrophy and long lasting results if the muscles are kept paralyzed. There is no limit to the number of times you get Botox, but as you age the effects may seem less and less because of the thinning skin.

Global skin care is important to combine with Botox treatments for facial lines and wrinkles. (Andrew P. Trussler, MD, Austin Plastic Surgeon)
