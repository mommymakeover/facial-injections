Botox Maximum Effect
The results of Botox Cosmetic injections begin to appear just a few short days after treatment.

Botox takes a few days to a week to begin showing its effects with the maximum effect at 2 weeks. The effect lasts about 3 months. Botox has an augmentative effect in that the more injections you have the better the result over time. (Paul Vitenas, Jr., MD, Houston Plastic Surgeon)

Botox usually has its maximum effect within 7-10 days, with no limit to the number of treatments

In most patients, Botox will begin to show results in about 3-5 days, and sometimes even earlier. The majority of patients will see the maximum effect within 7-10 days, with the effects typically lasting from 2-4 months.

Botox Effect Is Obtained Two Weeks After The Injections

There is no defined limit to the number of treatments that a patient can receive, and many patients have been safely using Botox for many years or even decades without any harmful effects. (Matthew Richardson, MD, Frisco Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Botox starts to take effect and most patients see resultsafter 3 to 4 days. However, I tell my patients maximum results are seen at 2weeks. It is best to have Botox treatments every 3-4 months, as it willgradually wear off within that time frame. (Melda A. Isaac, MD, Washington DC Dermatologic Surgeon)

Botox longevity

Botox typically starts working between 3-7 days and is maximally effective after 10-14 days. It lasts depending on the patient, the location, and the dose between 2-4 or more months.

You can receive Botox again after your current treatment is wearing off and there is no lifetime limit to the overall number of times you get Botox. (Lesley Rabach, MD, New York Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Dr Jordan Rihani, MD, Southlake Facial Plastic Surgeon – 33 Year Old Woman Treated With Botox

Botox timeline

After Botox injections, you will start seeing results in 2-3 days with maximum results in 2 weeks. The Botox wears off in about 3-4 months. I have many patients who have been getting Botox for 5-10 years now. (Mandy Lynn Warthan, MD, Dallas Dermatologist)

Results from your Botox injections can be seen after 2-3 days. The maximum results can be seen at 2 weeks. (Hardik Soni, MD (not currently practicing), Summit Emergency Medicine Physician)

Botox Results in 7 – 10 days

The average patient will start to see Botox results in as few as five days following injections. However, the maximum effect is usually seen in seven to ten days. Gradual return or “wearing off” of the product is seen in three to four months in most patients. (Rod J. Rohrich, MD, Dallas Plastic Surgeon)

Dr Lynn Chiu-Collins, MD, San Francisco Facial Plastic Surgeon – Botox (or Dysport) For Jaw Slimming

Botox results

Botox results typically take 3-10 days to appear, and the maximum benefit is usually seen at 2 weeks. (Anatoli Freiman, MD, Toronto Dermatologist)

No known limit on Botox injections

Most patients after Botox injections start to see results in 2-3 days and maximum results in about one week. At this time there is no known limit on how many Botox injections you can get.

Many patients have gotten Botox religiously every 3-4 months for 7-8 years without any negative repercussions. (James McMahan, MD, Columbus Plastic Surgeon)

Results of BOTOX

Dr Sirish Maddali, MD, Portland Plastic Surgeon – Botox For Crows Feet

Most of my patients start to notice the effects of BOTOX in about 4-5 days with the maximum effect at 14 days.

It lasts for 3 months so you can have a repeat treatment in 3 months.

Many of my patients who come every 3 months find that they can make the interval longer between treatments over time.

As you age, you will need more units of Botox to decrease the inevitable increasing lines. (Jean Keamy, MD, Westborough Ophthalmologist)

Botox can start working in 24 hours

In studies, it is shown that Botox can start to become effective in as little as 24 hours, and maximum benefit occurs by 7 days. Most of our patients start getting Botox every 3 months, then after a few treatments, we extend it to every 4 months.

The Maximum Effect Of Botox Usually Occurs At About 10-14 Days

Many can extend it even further to every 5 or even 6 months. Botox has been used for decades and as far as we know, it is safe to use on a regular basis forever. (Andrew Campbell, MD, Milwaukee Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Botox takes effect after about 2-3 days. But will not reach its maximum effect for up to 2 weeks. (Jeffrey M. Joseph, MD, Newport Beach Oculoplastic Surgeon)

Every patient reacts to Botox in a different way. In general, you may start to see some changes on day 3, but maximum results may take between 7-14 days. I tend to evaluate my Botox patients at 2 weeks to see if a touch-up is needed. (Jose Rodriguez-Feliz, MD, Miami Plastic Surgeon)

Typically, patients begin to see softening of fine lines within the first 4-7 days after injection with the maximum effect occurring by 2 weeks after receiving Botox.

Dr. Asif Pirani, MD, FRCS(C), Toronto Plastic Surgeon – Botox – Masseter Reduction

My office sees patients 2 weeks after their appointment to evaluate and make any necessary “touch ups.”

Botox can last up to 4 months, and this varies by patient. Because Botox temporarily improves the look of fine lines and frown lines, maintenance is required. (Aldo Guerra, MD, Scottsdale Plastic Surgeon)

You can start to see some signs of your neuromodulator working in 2-3 days. Our studies have shown that this vary slightly depending on a few variables: the location that you had injected, whether you are a man or a woman, and even which type of neuromodulator you had injected – Botox versus Dysport versus Xeomin.

You definitely need to wait a couple weeks to truly judge the effect of your neuromodulator, and it’s maximum effect will peek around four weeks. (Cameron Chesnut, MD, FAAD, FACMS, Spokane Dermatologic Surgeon)

Patients see the initial results starting at about day 2 after the Botox injections and maximum effect is at day 7. Depending upon the muscle structure comment amount of units injected Botox usually lasts anywhere from 4-8 months. (William Portuese, MD, Seattle Facial Plastic Surgeon)
