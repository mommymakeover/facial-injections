What Are Botox Bunny Lines?

Bunny lines are mainly caused by excessive movement of the nasalis muscle, an inverted horse-shoe shaped muscle at the top of the nose. Ironically these lines and horizontal lines just above them can be caused by Botox, and are sometimes called Botox lines.

They are rather simple to treat, in experienced hands. This treatment is inexpensive, only requiring 6-9 units, costing roughly $50-100. I say in experienced hands because there is a slim muscle running along the bottom of this region which attaches to the lips.

Tampering with this muscle can lead to a crooked smile. Also there are some decently sized blood vessels coursing lower than the target area and if these are hit there can be some fairly nasty bruising and swellling.

Botox is excellent to eliminate Bunny Lines

Most people think that Botox can only help the frown lines between the brows. But people hate the lines that come off at an angle from the eyes onto the nose, commonly known as Bunny Lines.

Those angle lines and horizontal creases on the bridge of the nose are all caused by muscle contraction. Botox injections into those areas can work wonders to reduce or relax the lines completely.

The best part is that a minimal amount of Botox is necessary to correct the problem, so the cost per unit is not excessive. (David A. Dreyfuss, MD, Orland Park Plastic Surgeon)
