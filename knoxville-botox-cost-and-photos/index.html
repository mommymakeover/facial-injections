About Botox injections know now, probably, almost everything. But, for sure, not all the details. Let’s try to answer the most popular questions on this topic.

What is Botox?

What is called botox is a neurotoxin type A, which produces the microorganism Clostridium botulinum. It is a toxin of protein nature, which is familiar to many under the name of botulinum toxin.

How does it work?

The skin on the face is directly connected to the underlying muscles. Therefore, when facial expressions are formed wrinkles on the forehead, nose, around the eyes.

If mimic muscles are blocked, the skin above them restores elasticity, wrinkles are smoothed. Neurotoxin just blocks the transfer of motor impulse from the nerve to the muscle fiber.

Botox Basically Prevents The Nerves There From Allowing Signals To Get Through

After the introduction of the drug, there is a pronounced relaxation of the facial muscles. But muscle atrophy is not observed, as their blood supply remains the same.

Long relaxation of tense facial muscles, on the one hand, leads to a smoothing of the skin folds, on the other hand, it gives the person the opportunity to get used to controlling the expression of the face.

Why do some cosmetologists use the term “Botox”, and others – “dysport”?

These are just different brand names of the same drug. Botox is produced by the American company Allergan, and the disport is a brand of the French company Beafour-lpsen-Speywood.

The real difference between the two drugs is only in terms of storage and the number of active units in the vial. Portability, efficacy, duration of action, the presence of adverse reactions in both drugs is the same.

Botox Requires No Surgery

What can not be done before the Botox injection?

The day before the injection, you can not drink alcohol – it can increase the risk of bruising and bruising. It is advisable to stop taking antibiotics and anticoagulants. In addition, beauticians recommend on the day of Botox injection do not tilt your head for a long time.

If the head is lowered for a long time, the circulation in the face will increase, and the drug may not be distributed correctly. For this reason, it is not recommended, for example, to attend massage sessions, during which you will have to lie down face, iron, vacuum, wash the floor (due to the need to keep the head in a tilted state) and buy shoes (fitting is also usually associated with constant slopes head).

Botox Takes Very Little Time To Administer

What can not be done immediately after the procedure?

It is strictly forbidden to touch the injection sites and massage them. In addition, several hours after the injection can not lie, play sports and take antibiotics and analgesics. Within 7-10 days after the procedure, it is recommended to exclude alcohol and overheating (hot baths, sauna, sauna). What can and should be done after the injection?

Botox injections are often called “lunch break procedure”, so immediately after the procedure you can get behind the wheel or go to work. However, to ensure that the result of injections does not disappoint you, within 4 hours after the procedure you need to be in an upright position and constantly strain your facial muscles within 24 hours.

How quickly becomes noticeable effect of the procedure?

Botox Treatment Keeps Your Forehead Muscles In A Relaxed State

The first result of the injection becomes noticeable after 5-7 days, when the muscles gradually begin to relax. On average, the maximum therapeutic effect is shown on day 14-15. There are observations of a very fast action – already on 2-3 days, and delayed for 3-4 weeks.

When is botox most effective?

Using botox, you can eliminate vertical wrinkles between the eyebrows (this is considered the most effective), horizontal wrinkles on the forehead, lowered eyebrows, eliminate oblique wrinkles on the side surfaces of the bridge of the nose, on the back of the nose (wrinkles of anger) and “crow’s feet” at the outer corner of the eye. But to smooth the wrinkles on the eyelids, smooth the nasolabial folds, eliminate the vertical wrinkles above and under the lips, smooth the vertical and horizontal wrinkles of the neck with the help of one botox is unreal. This requires an integrated approach.

In addition, in some cases, injections in the lower third of the face can lead to unpredictable consequences (possibly the omission of soft tissues).

At what age the injections can be done?

In principle, botox is permitted for use in cosmetology purposes at the age of 18 to 65 years. It is best to make injections from 30-33 years of age, when due to the decrease in the elasticity of the skin, mimic wrinkles begin to appear intensively. But if at an earlier age you constantly wrinkle, then with a preventive purpose it can be done earlier. At the age of 60 years, Botox should be used very carefully, in any case it is necessary to begin rejuvenation with surgical lifting (neurotoxin helps to get rid of facial wrinkles, but not able to eliminate excess skin).

Note that Botox is effective only for facial wrinkles (horizontal folds in the forehead, crow’s feet and vertical folds in the lips), because it does not allow the facial muscles to shrink.

But it does not work, for example, against fine wrinkles due to a change in the relief of the skin and wrinkles around the mouth due to the flabbiness of the cheeks. Wrinkle smoothing starts approximately 24 hours after the injection and lasts for 5 days. The positive effect can last from 3 to 12 months, but on average it lasts 6 months.

Let’s return once again to the question of the safety of Botox injections.

For example, if Botox is injected very close to the upper eyelid, then the eyelid can drop. The restoration of this defect takes 2-3 months.

Injection in the forehead can lead to inability to raise an eyebrow. Botox may also cause an allergic reaction.

Remember the contraindications to the injection:

  • Blood clotting disorders and keloid type of skin scarring.
  • Pregnancy and lactation.
  • Skin infection in the area of the proposed injection (dermatitis, neurodermatitis, psoriasis, eczema, etc.).
  • Previous plastic surgery in the eyelid region.
