How Many Units Botox For 11s?

Botox is a purified protein used to address wrinkles associated with facial expressions. The pivotal studies on Botox demonstrated that when 20 units is placed in the glabella between the brows, most people enjoy their results for 3-4 months.

For this reason in my practice I typically start with 20 units in this area the first time and follow up in two weeks. At that time more can always be added.

I have never used more than 40 units in this area. Dysport is another neuromodulator used in this area. 50-60 units of Dysport is the equivalent to 20 units of Botox.

While I understand that cosmetic treatments are an investment I have seen many patients who have had negative experiences with daily deal sites.

50 Units Botox Is Too Much As A First Glabella Treatment

The first question I ask is if one would look for the cheapest haircut. If not, then why would one look for the cheapest Botox, which has more risks than a haircut.

From the business end, many offices actually lose money when offering such deals and a number of practices must be used to recover this loss.

Some offices offer plastic surgery; in this case one surgery can easily recover the loss of many Botox treatments.

Other places offer more units, offer treating other areas, and offer other treatments such as lasers and fillers.

I have even seen some offices convince patients to use a different neuromodulator which costs the office less (but can still achieve the desired results). (Alex Eshaghian, MD, PhD, Encino Physician)

Botox Dosing Glabella

Botox is generally well tolerated in the Glabella area with about 20 units. (Michele S. Green, MD, New York Dermatologist)

Botox between the brows

A typical starting dose for botox to the glabellar area (between the eyebrows) is 25 units. This is for Botox Cosmetic (onabotulinum toxin) and Xeomin (incobotulinum toxin).

Dysport is dosed differently (higher). 50 units for Botox Cosmetic or Xeomin is too high as a starting dose. (Katherine Zamecki, MD, FACS, Danbury Oculoplastic Surgeon)

50 Units of Botox is usually enough to do between your brows, forehead and your crows feet. If you are just interested in treating between your brows, it typically takes approximately 20 units of Botox. Units can be different if the injector is using Dysport. (Lawrence Bundrick, MD, Huntsville Plastic Surgeon)
