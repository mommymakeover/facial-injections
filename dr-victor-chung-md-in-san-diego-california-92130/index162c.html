Is my nose pinched and over-resected? How to fix it?
Rhinoplasty: structure vs reductive.
Feb 3, 2019
Rhinoplasty is a surgery to improve the shape of your nose for breathing or appearance. A reductive rhinoplasty is one in which bumps and extra tissue is removed to make the nose smaller. If the nose is over-reduced, tissue (cartilage) needs to be added to create support and shape, this is called a structure rhinoplasty. With additional support, pinching and collapsing may be avoided for a safe and happy result. Safety comes first.

Over the years my nose doesn’t have that little point anymore – what can be done?
Rhinoplasty: closed vs open approach
Feb 3, 2019
Rhinoplasty is a surgery to improve the shape of the nose for breathing or appearance. Closed or endonasal approach means all the cuts are inside the nostrils. An open or external approach means there is a cut on the bridge of skin between your nostrils (columella). I would have a discussion with your surgeon regarding the incision they will use prior to your surgery, so you know what to expect. Both approaches are reasonable for a safe and happy result.
