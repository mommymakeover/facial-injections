Will PRP microneedling and Fraxel laser every month be helpful to get rid of these scars?
Acne scars
Jan 15, 2021
Your acne scars may benefit from a procedure called TCA CROSS. It requires multiple sessions and helps build collagen directly beneath the scar. I would also consider using a laser such as the Fraxel laser or CO2 laser to hep blend the scar into the surrounding skin. These lasers build collagen faster than the microneedling procedures. Speak with a board-certified dermatologist to learn more. Best of luck!

I would like a second opinion: What can be done about post fillers under-eye “bruising”?
Tyndall effect
Jan 15, 2021
Filler when placed too superficially especially under the eyes can create what’s called the Tyndall effect. Basically, the light scattering within the gel can create a blue-ish hue which can look like a bruise. I recommend you go back to your original injector and ask to have some of it dissolved. Hope this helps!

Does it matter whether you use a needle or cannula to fill in acne scars with dermal filler on the ch
Needle vs. cannula for acne scars
Nov 13, 2019
Good question! In my practice, I find needles work better for treating acne scars. I first use the needle tip to break up scar tissue that’s tethered to the underlying skin (a procedure called subcision). Once a small pocket is created, I inject filler to help pop up the acne scar so that it is flush with the normal, surrounding skin. Hope that helps!
Would fillers work in these “dents”, under my lips, or would it look odd?
Filler for lower face
Nov 13, 2019
Yes, injecting a filler such as Juvederm Ultra Plus or Restylane Lyft to the prejowl hollowing you’re describing would look really nice. You might also consider adding a neuromodulator such as Botox or Dysport to your chin muscle (mentalis) to help soften the fine dimpling of this muscle. Be sure to consult with your physician regarding which treatment is right for you.
Can you get facial filler and get laser hair removal on the face?
Voluma and laser hair removal
Nov 13, 2019
Hi Sebastian,
Great question! Voluma is typically injected deep under the skin to give a lifting effect. The energy from laser hair removal devices does not reach the depth where the filler would be placed. You should ask the physician who will be injecting the filler as well as the one performing the laser hair removal for their recommendations. I generally recommend spacing the two treatments about 1-2 weeks apart from each other. Hope this helps!
