Botox Prices And Photos In Louisville KY

The word Botox is known to everyone. When it comes to cosmetic manipulations with the face and procedures of rejuvenation, everyone immediately remembers about botox.

But does botox really save you from aging and wrinkles?

Botox is the name of the drug produced by the American company Allergan. The active ingredient is purified botulinum toxin.

What is Botox?

Botulinoxin is a purified protein that is designed to remove muscle spasm. Since 1989 Botox has been actively used in medicine in the fight against diseases that cause muscle spasms.

The essence of the work of Botox is that, getting into the muscle, it blocks its susceptibility to nerve impulses, and at a certain concentration, it completely immobilizes the muscle.

Botox Botulinum Toxin Type A By Dr. Garrett M. Crabtree, Louisville Plastic Surgeon

What is botox used for?

Botox is used to combat mimic wrinkles. Note, not with age-related wrinkles, namely with facial wrinkles. After all, this type of wrinkles appears because of the excessively active work of the muscular musculature.

Let’s see how the mimic wrinkles arise: You move the muscles of the face, forming folds on the skin. Over time, these folds cease completely to smooth out, and wrinkles form. At first it is not deep, however, soon its depth will increase.

Botox is also used for correction of the lips, nose, face oval and regulation of the sebaceous glands.

How does botox work?

Speaking of cosmetology, botox penetrates the muscle of the face, where there is a facial wrinkle and blocks its susceptibility. Due to this action, the muscle relaxes and ceases to contract. As a result, the facial wrinkle becomes smaller, and if it was shallow – disappears altogether.

Also injections of botox into this or that muscle in the first stages of the formation of wrinkles can help wean, squint, or wrinkle the forehead. Just for the duration of the Botox you will not learn how to do it, keeping yourself young.

Botox Is Injected With A Fine Needle Into Specific Muscles

The duration of the drug on the muscle is 5-6 months. It is noteworthy that the drug has no age restrictions, as many think. It should be applied as needed, as soon as you notice the formation of facial wrinkles. Moreover, if there are already many wrinkles on the face, botox will only worsen the situation and the skin will sag. Remember, facial wrinkles do not appear due to changes in the skin fibers, as it does during aging, but because of the mobility of the muscles.

Myth 1: Botox distorts facial expression

Some psychologists believe that if, for example, to smooth out wrinkles around the eyes, your smile may seem to be surrounding the fake and insincere, which will necessarily affect the communication. Specialists in Botox therapy claim: an undesirable restriction or distortion of facial expression can occur only in case of illiterate selection of the dose of the drug or the site of injection.

Myth 2: Botox may be poisonous

You can not hide the truth: botox is really a weak solution of botulinum toxin – the same poison that is formed in improperly prepared canned mushrooms and causes a terrible disease of botulism. When it enters the muscle, this substance causes it to relax, blocking the transmission of nerve impulses. The safety of Botox is based on the principle of point introduction to a particular muscle.

Moreover, the concentration of toxin in the preparation is so small that it will simply not have enough power to spread beyond the injection site. So the right dosage and accurate professional injection is a guarantee of safety. According to doctors, aspirin and antibiotics are potentially much more dangerous than botox.

Botox Relaxes And Softens Overactive Facial Muscles That Cause Wrinkles And Lines

Myth 3: Botox “causes addiction”

The drug does not cause physiological dependence. Especially since the action of Botox is temporary: in 3-6 months the muscle will “recover” and begin to contract as usual. Now about the dependence of psychological.

After Botox injection for a long time, facial wrinkles are smoothed out, that is, 3-6 months a person looks and feels younger and more attractive. Of course, this condition can not not like.

When the effect of Botox comes to naught, the person has already got used to the good and does not want his “crow’s feet” and all the other wrinkles coming back.

What does a person do? He makes a new injection! Should I call the desire to look good psychological dependence? It is a philosophical question.

There is another important aspect. Since after the “injection of beauty” a person for a long time can not use some facial muscles, then in due course his mimic habits can change. For example, that for a year a person “forgets” how to frown or wrinkle his forehead.

Myth 4: Botox is a stress for muscles

In fact, everything is exactly the opposite. We laugh, cry, wrinkle our nose or frown, and mimic muscles work in the meantime. Even in a dream, we often can not completely relax the face. Over the years, fatigue accumulates …

Agree, a couple of years without a vacation and we want to scream from overexertion. And our muscles without a smile were smiling and frowning for more than a decade. Why not give them a break? Botox injection forcibly relaxes muscles, and they, like in a sanatorium, enjoy the long-awaited peace.

Bruising Is The Most Common Side Effect

Myth 5: Botox injection is painful

This is another common misconception. Botox injection is painless: the drug and injections do not cause unpleasant sensations either during or after. Most of the people who have experienced botox injection compare their sensations from a shot with a mosquito bite.

Moreover, before the procedure begins, the doctor applies an anesthetic cream on the patient’s face or freezes the injection site with ice. The only consequence that a patient may encounter is a tiny bruise at the injection site. But even this happens very rarely.

Myth 6: Botox can be done only after 40 years of age

Doctors believe that Botox injections can be done starting from the age of eighteen, if, of course, there is a need. It’s not a secret for anyone that even teenagers have deep mimic wrinkles …
