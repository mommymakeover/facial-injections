Can I Have Botox When Breastfeeding?

Botox and breastfeeding

Because there has been no studies to establish the safety of Botox during pregnancy or during breastfeeding, it is probably best to wait until after done breastfeeding.

However, numerous women have accidentally gotten their Botox treatment prior to them knowing they were pregnant without any problems.

The same is true for women who have gotten it done while breastfeeding. However until studies are performed, the best advice it to wait. (Steven E. Rasmussen, MD, FAAD, Austin Dermatologist)

Botox safe after pregnancy

Botox is safe to use after delivery, however, if you are breast feeding it is recommended that you do not use Botox.

There are no known adverse events to a breast feeding infant as a result of Botox to mom while breast feeding, however, there are no controlled studies.

A Toxin That When Injected Into Muscles Causes Temporary Paralysis

It is known that a great number of women have ignored this warning and received Botox while breast feeding. The same holds true for receiving Botox during pregnancy. There have been no studies, there have been women who have purposely or accidentally received Botox while pregnant, and there have been no reports of fetal injury from these imporper uses of Botox. (Louis W. Apostolakis, MD, Austin Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Do Not Use During Breast-Feeding

Although there have not been any case studies on whether one should use Botox during pregnancy or after, I highly recommend that you should not use Botox while you are pregnant or breastfeeding. (Sanusi Umar, MD, Redondo Beach Dermatologic Surgeon)

Botox should not be used during breast-feeding

In general, Botox can be resumed soon after child birth as long as you are not breast-feeding. However, you should not use Botox or Dysport if you are still breast-feeding. (Sherry Li, MD, PhD, New York Dermatologist)

Botox And Breast Feeding

Botox and pregnancy has not been studied extensively because of the risks to particular test subjects and more so their babies. I would avoid it until breast feeding is complete. I would also not use botox during pregnancy.

Why take a chance? (Chris Thiagarajah, MD, Denver Oculoplastic Surgeon)

While Botox is definitely not recommended during pregnancy, its use in breastfeeding has not been studied. Botox is pregnancy category C, which means there is some evidence that it may cause harm to the baby.

How long after pregnancy before Botox injections

Botox injections should not be done while breastfeeding. There are no studies done as to whether Botox is is safe during breastfeeding, and although the likelihood is small, it’s not zero that some of it may get into the milk and be ingested by the baby.

Therefore, it is best to wait with Botox injections until you are finished breastfeeding. Otherwise there are no time restrictions as to how soon after pregnancy you can have Botox injections. (Emily Altman, MD, Short Hills Dermatologic Surgeon)
