Does Botox Take Time To Work?

The effects of Botox can usually be seen by 5-7 days after injection, although full effects may take up to 2 weeks. If after 2 weeks, you feel that you haven’t received the full benefit, talk to your doctor as you may simply need more to achieve the desired effect.

The number of times that you can receive Botox is unlimited, but your face will continue to age and eventually your best choice may be surgery. (John Zavell, MD, FACS, Toledo Plastic Surgeon)

Botox time to work

Botox takes 1 week to work. For some patients it works in 3-4 days some takes 7-8 days. It lasts for at 3 months. (Avron Lipschitz, MD, Stuart Plastic Surgeon)

Botox should last about four months. I have been injecting Botox for over 15 years, and I have some long term patients who now need Botox about every six months.

Dr Kim Nichols, MD, Greenwich Dermatologist – 64-year-old Before And After Botox For Crow’s Feet

I think with their continued use of Botox, their muscles have been permanently weakened. (Stephan Finical, MD, Charlotte Plastic Surgeon)

How long before botox works after injection?

Botox, Dysport and Xeomin all take approximately 5-7 days for full effect. If you have been receiving one of these injectables regularly it can be faster, some patients report as fast as 2 days. The response to each medication is variable, certain patients feel that one of the medications works faster than others in them.

I tell my patients that the toxin inhibits for about 3-4 months, but I have had a few patients that it lasts only 2 months, and a lucky few that can go 5 months between injections, so it does vary slightly.

Botox has been available in the U. S. for cosmetic purposes for over 10 years and I have patients that have been receiving it every 3-4 months for almost that entire time without problems.

How Safe Is Botox As A Cosmetic Procedure

At this time there is no restriction on how long you can continue to receive Botox, Dysport, or Xeomin injections. (Jennifer Janiga, MD, Reno Dermatologic Surgeon)

Botox Is Fast and Effective

The majority of my patients report they notice a difference 3-4 days after their Botox treatment; however, in some patients it may take a week post Botox treatment to notice results.

Depending on how severe the patients lines are will help depict when “full benefits” are achieved.

For deep lines, that are visible even when the face is relaxed, it may take multiple treatments to reduce their appearance. There are no limitations on the amount of times a patient can receive Botox.

As long as your are healthy, not pregnant or breast feeding, etc. continuing to receive Botox is fine.

You shouldn’t expect immediate results from botox injections

Also, for many people Botox treatments are scheduled in 4 month increments; however, for some it could be more frequent or less frequent than this.

Everyone is different. Some patients have stronger facial muscles than others, some metabolize Botox faster than others, and some use more facial movement during expression than others.

This is personalized and you and your injector should discuss the proper time frame for your individually. (Peter J. Capizzi, MD, Charlotte Plastic Surgeon)

Botox usually starts working after a couple of days but does not reach its full result for nearly two weeks. It typically lasts for 3-4 months.

Each person is a little different. It is possible that you can start to lengthen the time from one treatment to another after you have used it for a while.

Most People Will Have Seen The Full Results After 14 Days

Make sure that you go to a qualified surgeon’s office and receive real Botox. Your ideal result should be a refreshed look but not overdone. (Rodger Shortt, FRCSC, Toronto Plastic Surgeon)

Botox Results, benefit, result, etc.

It varies, typically it takes between 3-7 days to start working and up to 2 weeks to achieve it’s maximum effect. That is of course if your initial treatment was an adequate dose. On average, people can get Botox every 3 months.

When the movement it back then it’s time to be retreated. There is no limit to how long a person can keep getting Botox as well. (Brian C. Reuben, MD, Salt Lake City Plastic Surgeon)

Three to ten days

Usually you will begin to see results with in 3 days and full effect at 10 days. If any corrections need to be done 14 days should be the right time to do this. (Tal T. Roudner, MD, FACS, Miami Plastic Surgeon)

Dr Jason Emer, MD, Los Angeles Dermatologic Surgeon – 45 Year Old Male Treated For Forehead Lines

Results in 3-7 days

Some patients report results with Botox immediately or in one day but that is normally due to some minor swelling. True results appear gradually over 3-7 days and the full benefit is for most people within that range, but results vary from patient to patient.

I usually counsel my patients in the use of Botox in the first year for it to be used every 3-4 months so that the muscles can get weaker. If you stick to this regimen then the results can last longer with each treatment and less units are often used.

The muscles get weaker with time and prevent wrinkles from forming. I have had patients go 9 months between injections and have had Botox for 10+ years. Botox is safe for long-term use. (Kristina Tansavatdi, MD, Los Angeles Facial Plastic Surgeon)
