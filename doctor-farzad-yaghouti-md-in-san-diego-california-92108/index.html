Can Botox cause ptosis or permanent muscle paralysis?
Botox effect on lids
Mar 27, 2015
Hi, I recommend that you do not get another Botox injection and go see an ophthalmologist to evaluate your eyelid function. As frequent Botox injections can lead to a frontalis muscle atrophy, it is possible that may lead to a permanent lowering of the eyebrow and the eyelids. Best, Dr. Yaghouti

Will PRK surgery correct both nearsightedness and farsightedness?
Will PRK surgery correct both nearsightedness and farsightedness
Mar 27, 2015
Hi Dixie, You cannot be both nearsighted and farsighted, unless you are nearsighted in one eye and farsighted in the other eye. Some patients may erroneously term a condition called "Presbyopia" (inability to read due to a gradual hardening of the lens inside the eye with aging, typically affecting patients around the age of 40 to 45 and worsening with age) as "farsightedness". If being presbyopic is what you mean by being "farsighted', then PRK is not going to fix that. Best, Dr....
Can anything be done to avoid biofilm infection with fillers? Specifically, Restylane.
Biofilm infection with fillers
Mar 27, 2015
As long as your physician follows strict cleanliness guidelines for injection, there is no one particular filler that is more associated with biofilm contamination than the others. Limiting the frequency of skin puncture sites using a cannula may also lower the chance of contamination.
Does Botox improve smoker's lines and chin lines?
Botox improve smoker's lines
Mar 27, 2015
Botox has a small role in treating smoker's line. Botox may be used as a means to decondition the patient's habit of puckering her lips which in many is the primary reason for the formation of those lines in the first place, but it doesn't have a huge effect on eliminating those lines by itself as the amount injected tends to be very small. A more effective approach to treating those lines would be to use a combination of soft fillers along with fractional Laser or radiofrequency.
