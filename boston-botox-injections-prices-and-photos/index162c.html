What is Botox and how it is work?

The amazing properties of botulinum toxin (botox) and its effect on the skin was discovered quite accidentally in 1982, when clinical studies were conducted in the treatment of strabismus and blepharospasm.

In the treatment of these conditions, its “side effect” in the form of smoothing of transverse wrinkles in the forehead was noted. The drug itself is a weakened botulinum toxin, which is produced by the life of the bacterium Clostridium botulinum.

This bacterium is the causative agent of a dangerous disease called botulism. When ingested, toxins can block the work of the muscles, causing their paralysis.

For several years this property of botox has been used in the treatment of diseases of the nervous system, but now injections of botox are widely used for aesthetic and cosmetic purposes.

Control injection under the skin of small doses of botulinum toxin can cause local paralysis of facial wrinkles, which lasts for several months, after which botox is excreted from the body.

Pros and Cons of Botox Injection

Do or not do Botox? Some say that the drug quickly eliminates wrinkles and signs of aging, while others discuss the harmful properties of botox. In order to decide to conduct such a procedure on your face, you need to know all the pros and cons of the method. When Botox is injected under the skin, facial wrinkles relax, and, to be more precise, the fibers of the muscles that form wrinkles relax. As a result, a person can no longer frown.

This property of the neurotoxin is used in those areas of the face in which the first signs of aging first appear.

These include the forehead, the zone between the eyebrows, the periorbital and perioral regions.

In addition, it was observed that injections of botulinum toxin into the axillary basins, palms and feet reduce the separation of sweat, which has become widely used in the treatment of hyperhidrosis.

Indications and contraindications for the use of Botox

Indication for the introduction: age changes, signs of aging (sagging) of the skin, as well as the presence of facial wrinkles.

There are also contraindications for Botox injections, given below:

What Areas Of The Face Can Be Treated With Botox

  • Period of pregnancy (lactation).
  • Muscle weakness in the area of administration.
  • Presence of chronic diseases.
  • Acute and chronic infections.
  • Skin diseases in places of injections.
  • Individual intolerance and hypersensitivity to Botox.

Consequences, side effects, complications

Usually, unpleasant sensations and possible complications appear immediately in the first few days after injections, but fortunately, the drug is completely eliminated from the body, and all the troubles are reversible.

Of all the complications and side effects noted, the following undesirable consequences of botox can be distinguished:

  • Spasm of the eyelids.
  • Ptosis of eyebrows.
  • Subcutaneous hemorrhage.
  • Disturbance of mobility of the upper lip (with deep administration of the drug).
  • Headache.
  • Puffiness at the injection site.
  • Technique of drug administration

Botox injections are carried out only after consulting a medical cosmetologist and identifying contraindications to the administration of the drug. It is important to assess the state of mimicry and the treatment area, so that after the session the face does not become immobile.

The skin is previously disinfected with an antiseptic solution, and places with increased sensitivity are treated with local anesthetics. After the procedure, you can immediately return to work, since injections of the drug do not require local anesthesia.
