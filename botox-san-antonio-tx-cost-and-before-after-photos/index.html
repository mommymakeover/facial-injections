About Botox injections know now, probably, almost everything. But certainly not all the details. Let’s try to answer the most popular questions on this topic.

What is Botox?

What is called botox is a neurotoxin type A that produces the microorganism Clostridium botulinum. It is a toxin of protein nature, which is familiar to many under the name of botulinum toxin.

How does it work?

The skin on the face is directly connected to the underlying muscles. Therefore, when facial expressions are formed wrinkles on the forehead, nose, around the eyes.

If mimic muscles are blocked, the skin above them restores elasticity, wrinkles are smoothed. Neurotoxin just blocks the transfer of motor impulse from the nerve to the muscle fiber. After the introduction of the drug, there is a pronounced relaxation of the facial muscles.

But muscle atrophy is not observed, as their blood supply remains the same. Long relaxation of tense facial muscles, on the one hand, leads to a smoothing of the skin folds, on the other hand, it gives the person the opportunity to get used to controlling the expression of the face.

Why do some cosmetologists use the term “botox” and other “disport”?

These are just different brand names of the same drug. Botox is produced by the American company Allergan, and the disport is a brand of the French company Beafour-Ipsen-Speywood. The real difference between the two drugs is only in the conditions of storage and the number of active units in the vial. Portability, efficacy, duration of action, the presence of adverse reactions in both drugs is the same.

Botox Is A Well Tolerated Procedure

How long has botox appeared and where is it most popular?

It was first discovered in the 19th century as a substance that causes botulism. Subsequently, it was purified and was successfully applied in medicine, and since 1980 – in cosmetology. In the United States, it is difficult to meet a woman who at least once did not inject Botox (and most do them all the time).

But Muslim women, it seems, will have to give up this method of fighting wrinkles. The main religious body of Malaysia has recently made the decision that it is unacceptable for the orthodox to inject Botox because some substances used in its manufacture are derived from pork that is considered unclean in Islam.

What is prohibited before Botox injection?

Botox Is An Effective Treatment For Wrinkles And Lines Related To Expression

The day before the injection, you can not drink alcohol – it can increase the risk of bruising and bruising. It is advisable to stop taking antibiotics and anticoagulants. In addition, beauticians recommend on the day of Botox injection do not tilt your head for a long time. If the head is lowered for a long time, the circulation in the face will increase, and the drug may not be distributed correctly.

For this reason, it is not recommended, for example, to attend massage sessions, during which you have to lie down face, iron, vacuum and wash the floor (due to the need to keep the head in a tilted state) and buy shoes (fitting is also usually associated with constant slopes ).

How does the procedure work?

Usually the patient is seated in a chair in a semi-reclining position. After the disinfection of the skin, subcutaneously or intramuscularly (depending on the area of administration) a thin short needle is injected with the drug.

On average, the procedure lasts 3-5 minutes. After injections for 10-15 minutes, ice is put on the skin.

Is it painful?

Correction of mimic wrinkles anesthesia, as a rule, does not require. Most patients compare this sensation with a mosquito bite. Although if you have a high threshold of sensitivity, you can ask for local anesthesia: a special cream with an anesthetic is applied to the skin. Immediately after the injections, you may feel a slight discomfort (heaviness in the muscles), but it quickly disappears.
