Who should inject Botox?

The first thing to recognize it that administering Botox is a medical procedure and as such should only be carried out by suitably qualified medical practitioners within a clinical environment.

Unfortunately, at present there is no standardization of training and no legislation, therefore to ensure a safe and high quality treatment you should seek treatment from a dedicated medical aesthetic practitioner, rather than someone who only carried out the procedures intermittently.

This could be some who is trained as a doctor, dentist or nurse. You can confirm they are qualified on the GMC, GDC or NMC registration list respectively. Who is qualified to inject Botox

Although Botox is commonly injected, it still is a drug and should be administered by properly trained health care personnel. These include physicians and providers working under a doctors supervision such as physician assistants, nurse practitioners, and nurses.

Make sure your health care provider can explain the risks to you, how Botox works, and why a certain dose is right for you. Ask your provider when and how they were trained to give Botox. (John Zavell, MD, FACS, Toledo Plastic Surgeon)
