Uneven hairline around the side. Could this be fixed?
Uneven Hairline
Apr 29, 2022
Thank you for your question. The hairline can be transplanted to even out any inherent unevenness along the temporal edges. The beauty of the transplant is that the new hairs can be designed to blend in with your existing hairline and achieve a natural look. Once the hairs have been transplanted and matured (typically at 12 months), the hairs can be styled and cropped. The hairs can be cut or cropped earlier, but they may appear less dense until the 12 month mark.
Will I still get new hair growth 8-10 months post-op?
Hair Growth 8-10 months Post Op
Apr 29, 2022
Thank you for your question. Each patient varies, however there is definitely still growth from 8-10 months post-op. Typically, a good gauge of the density can be appreciated at about the 9month mark. The final result isn't truly appreciated until 12 months. Certain areas such as the crown, tend to take a little longer and can be up to 18 months. The best way to appreciate the progress is to take photos in the same location every month.

Can I have hair transplant for bald spot I was born with on the temple?
Temple Hair Transplant
Apr 26, 2022
Thank you for sending pictures. The hair loss that you have on your temple since birth is most likely a condition called Temporal Triangular Alopecia (which is also known as Congenital Triangular Alopecia). It would be advised to first see a Dermatologist to confirm the diagnosis and rule out any other cause that could appear similar to it (such as Alopecia Areata). If it is truly Temporal Triangular Alopecia, then it can definitely be transplanted into successfully.
Will transplanted hair look more natural as it grows?
Natural Looking Results after 6 months
Apr 26, 2022
It is common for hairs to be appear spacier and "unnatural" looking at 6 months. This can happen up until they have matured, which takes one full year. The hairs will continue to grow and thicken over the course of the 12 months. The final result really can't be appreciated until then, so hang in there! If after 12 months, it hasn't thickened and appeared more dense, at that point you may consider a touch up procedure, but not before then.
