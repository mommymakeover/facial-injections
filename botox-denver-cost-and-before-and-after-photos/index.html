Botox Denver Cost And Before & After Photos

Botox is considered to be a popular preparation for wrinkle removal on the face. In fact, Botox in Denver is not a medicine, but, on the contrary, poison, only weakened and purified to such an extent that it can not cause harm to health.

In science, this agent is called the botulinum neurotoxin type A. The very word “Botox” means the American name of the drug. The French product is an analogue of Botox is called Dysport.

The history of the appearance of Botox

Bacteria that temporarily paralyzed the muscles (Clostridium botulinum) were discovered at the end of the 19th century, but more than 60 years passed before scientists came up with rational use for them.

Botox Is For Wrinkles Caused By The Use Of Facial Muscles

In the late 1960s, Edward Schantz and Alan Scott began to test botulinum on monkeys. Later, this substance began to treat excessive sweating, strabismus, blepharospasm and achalasia of the esophagus. At the turn of millennia, botulinum toxin began to be actively used in cosmetology.

Indications and contraindications

The substance can be used at the age of the patient up to 60 years. After that, injections do not bring the proper effect, because the formed wrinkles are in no way dependent on the activity of facial muscles.

Botox is best used at the age of 30 to 40 years. This is the period when wrinkles are already clearly drawn, but the artificial blockage of the corresponding muscles is still able to smooth the skin of the face. After the age of 40 years, no longer expect a staggering effect from the use of Botox in Denver.

Despite the relative safety, there are the following contraindications to Botox:

  • individual toxin intolerance;
  • hemophilia;
  • pregnancy and lactation;
  • myasthenia gravis and similar syndromes;
  • exacerbation of some infectious and viral diseases;
  • problem skin of the face at the site of the alleged injections (herpes sores, etc.);
  • reception of anticoagulants and antibiotics;
  • oncology.

Cost of the procedure

For each group of facial muscles, a certain amount of Botox is needed, the size of which is measured in units of action (not in grams). We will give approximate costs of material and prices:

  • fold between eyebrows – up to 25 units.
  • wrinkles on the forehead – up to 15 units.
  • corners of the eye – up to 16 units.

The price of Botox is approximately the same as the cost of Dysport. The cost of the procedure can be affected by the reputation of a specialist. But to resort to the help of amateurs for the sake of saving money is not worth it, because inept treatment of a toxin can mutilate a face for several months.

Botox Reaches A Facial Muscle Not Being Treated

Botox is administered by an outpatient physician or other person authorized to perform the procedure. Before the procedure, the face is treated with a disinfectant, as well as a special anesthetic cream. For injections, a syringe with a short and thin needle is used.

Botox is introduced into those muscles, the reduction of which is planned to be reduced to a minimum or temporarily paralyzed. The whole procedure lasts up to 10 minutes. After that, face skin is disinfected and cooled.

The onset of effect and duration of action of the drug

In most cases, the final result is visible after one or two weeks. By this time, the treated mimic muscles relax and completely or partially stop responding to nerve impulses. Some patients can see the result already on the second day, and someone will have to wait about a month.

Discomfort Is Minimal And Lasts Only A Few Seconds

The effect of the first procedure of rejuvenation lasts from 4 to 6 months. Then the muscles gradually “come to life” and again begin to actively move in response to human emotions. Therefore, for a long-lasting result, the procedure should be repeated, especially since with each subsequent procedure the effect will last longer (up to one year).

More clearly, the effect of botulinum toxin can be assessed by viewing the photos before and after Botox.

After the procedure

For better assimilation of the substance after the procedure, it is necessary to follow simple rules:

  • the first 3-4 hours not to sleep and not touch your face with your hands;
  • for two days exclude sports, swimming, hiking in the sauna and sauna;
  • in the early days avoid direct sunlight;

in the next day before the procedure to stay away from taking alcohol and drugs, in any way, affecting the coagulability of the blood.

Possible complications and harm

Complications after Botox, as a rule, arise due to the incompetence of the person performing injections. Too high a dose for a particular area of the skin can cause some facial muscles to lose shape. An effect called “ptosis of tissues” is obtained.

Uneven injection of drugs, for example, into the corners of the lips can lead to asymmetry and distortion of the mouth.

Fortunately, such errors are not eternal, but a certain period will have to “be content” with this kind. Therefore, the face before and after Botox does not always change for the better. In addition, in the first few days the face can be swollen, and the points of the injections can bleed slightly.

The Filler Will Have Its Own Timeline For Effectiveness

In addition to complications, many are interested in the question: what else is Botox dangerous? In America, where this drug has been used for several decades, scientists have noted rare, but cases of deterioration of mental activity, difficulty breathing, even deaths after the unsuccessful application of botulinum toxin.

As you can see, the fight against wrinkles with the help of Botox is quite safe. However, the expected effect and the actual result do not always coincide. The specialist has to be a jeweler to correctly calculate the injection site and the amount of botulinum toxin required. Therefore, to trust your face is desirable only experienced masters with a lot of positive feedback.
