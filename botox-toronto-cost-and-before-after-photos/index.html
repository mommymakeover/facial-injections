The first wrinkles are always unexpected. It would seem that a day ago the skin was like that of a baby, and here in the mirror we see a treacherous fold on his forehead or small “crow’s feet” around eyes.

Botox is a drug made on the basis of natural proteins, which are weakened botulinum neurotoxins.

Botox action is based on the fact that small doses of it, introduced under the skin, can relieve the tension of small muscles, as a result of which the mimic activity becomes less pronounced.

Neurotoxin, which is contained in botox, blocks the flow of signals from the motor nerves to the muscles, as a result of which the usual mimic reactions do not cause the characteristic movements of facial muscles of the face.

The selectivity of the toxin action is achieved by injecting the drug directly into those muscles whose movement should be blocked. Precision is achieved through the use of thin needles.

Botox began to be used in medicine in the early 50’s of the last century, after it was proven to be effective in reducing muscle activity. First, botox was used in ophthalmology – the administration of the drug proved to be a very effective way of treating strabismus.

Botox was then used to treat other eye diseases, and later Botox was used to treat conditions associated with motor disorders, as well as to treat such a terrible disease as Parkinson’s disease.

And only later came the idea to use botox as a cosmetic product: it was found that the introduction of the drug into the facial muscles prevents them from contracting, which, in turn, stops the deepening of existing wrinkles and the formation of new ones.

After the introduction of Botox muscle relaxes, resulting in the skin over them smoothed. How do Botox injections?

It should be clarified that the Botox injection is performed only in clinics by specialists who have the right to engage in medical activities. This type of service is not available in beauty salons.

Before the procedure, the doctor receives information about the patient’s state of health and finds out the contraindications for the administration of the drug. Preparation for the introduction of Botox also includes an examination of the skin condition.

After this, the doctor studies the features of facial expressions of the patient and determines the places of administration of the drug, marking them with a marker. The introduction of Botox requires local anesthesia, since without it, injections can be quite painful.

As an anesthetic, usually a cream is used. After that, with the help of a thin needle, botox is introduced into the facial muscles.

The entire procedure for taking the drug takes 10 to 20 minutes, it is performed on an outpatient basis. Immediately after the procedure, the patient can return to normal activities. How quickly the effect of the introduction of Botox and how long it lasts?

The patient will notice a positive result from Botox injections within three days. There is a pronounced smoothing of wrinkles, improving the complexion.

After this period, injections of the drug can be repeated – no addiction to it occurs. What should I do after the procedure?

Despite the fact that Botox injections are completely painless, patients should be prepared for the fact that after a procedure for several days they may experience some discomfort. Sometimes after injections of the drug on the face there are swelling and a slight swelling.

These phenomena are completely in a few days. In order for the recovery after injections of Botox to pass faster, and the result of its application was as strong as possible, it is recommended to adhere to the following rules:

  • Avoid the horizontal position of the body within the first 4 hours after the procedure of drug administration.
  • Within an hour after the introduction of Botox, the patient must completely avoid any facial movements.
  • It is prohibited to be exposed to the open sun for at least 3 days after Botox injections.
  • Within a week after the procedure is not recommended to visit the sauna, sauna or solarium.
  • Within 6 months after the course of Botox it is necessary to exclude the use of antibiotics, preparations containing calcium, and tranquilizers.

Is the effectiveness of Botox equally effective for different patients?

In the practice of Botox, it was found that the toxins contained in the drug do not affect all patients. Therefore, before carrying out the procedure, the doctor necessarily conducts a test: this will avoid unnecessary costs for the procedure, which may prove ineffective for this patient.

What is the best age for Botox injections?

Officially botox can be used for medicinal purposes for patients older than 9 years of age. As for botox injections, which are performed for cosmetic purposes, the expediency of starting them is determined for oneself.

Mimic wrinkles form throughout the life of a person, and in some cases they can be expressed at the age of 20 years. As practice shows, the most appropriate application of Botox in patients aged 25-65 years. Is botox dangerous?

Since botox is made on the basis of toxins, the question naturally arises: is not it dangerous to use botox for healthy people? The answer to this question is unambiguous: the use of Botox does not carry any danger to the health of the patient. Areas of application of Botox.

Most of us Botox is known only as a drug used in cosmetic medicine. But meanwhile the scope of Botox is much wider.

Botox is successfully used in the treatment of neurological diseases, muscular dystonia, in the treatment of neuritis, facial spasms, spasms of the muscles of other parts of the body.

In recent years, botox is also used as one of the drugs of post-traumatic recovery. Botox helps in the treatment of such a formidable disease as cerebral palsy, improving the bone and muscle tissue and removing pain syndromes.

Botox can also help with an unpleasant problem that many people have to face – hyperhidrosis, or excessive sweating. Botox injections equally well help to cope with sweating of the armpits, and with sweating of the feet and palms.

Complications and contraindications to the use of Botox. Since, the use of Botox for cosmetic purposes has been known for many years, the effect of the drug has been well studied and the risk of complications with Botox administration is minimal.

Extremely rare are such side effects as the lowering of the corner of the mouth or eyelid – their appearance is due to the inexperience of the manipulating doctor.

However, the action of Botox is temporary, so that any negative phenomena completely disappear after a few months.

In order to avoid complications associated with the introduction of Botox, all patients before the passage of the procedure must consult with a doctor.

The professionalism of the cosmetologist conducting the procedure allows to achieve optimal results of Botox use, which consider the disappearance of wrinkles while maintaining normal facial expressions.

Despite the fact that Botox injections are harmless to the body, there are a number of contraindications to the administration of the drug. Among them – pregnancy, hemophilia, skin inflammation in the area of drug administration, myasthenia gravis.
