Patients who have lost a lot of weight—either through a healthy diet and exercise or with the help of bariatric surgery—are frequently left with sagging skin. Along with potential hygiene and health issues, excess skin can distort their body’s true appearance and make it feel as if their weight loss journey isn’t yet finished.

That’s where Dr. Michaels’s renowned body sculpting skills come into play, with a customized combination of skin removal surgery and body contouring. Procedures may include liposuction (SmartLipo, VASER lipo, or tumescent liposuction), tummy tucks, breast lifts, arm lifts, or a full upper and/or lower body lift.

The depth of Dr. Michaels’s body contouring experience has made him an invaluable resource, often tapped as an expert by the media and scientific journals alike. Passionate about furthering advancements in plastic surgery, Dr. Michaels frequently performs research. He has been an investigator in FDA-approved clinical trials, most notably of tissue glue used in tummy tuck surgery (abdominoplasty).

In addition to being an assistant professor at Johns Hopkins School of Medicine in the department of plastic and reconstructive surgery, he helps train plastic surgery residents from Georgetown University and Johns Hopkins Hospitals.

Why are my arm lift scars so bad?
arm lift scars
Sep 3, 2018
The arm lift scars are some the slowest to fade as compared to other body contouring procedures. It can take up to 2 years to fade and you are just at the one year mark.
