How To Get Rid Of Bunny Lines?

Bugged by Bunny Lines?

Many of my patients are bothered by the “Bunny Lines” on the sides of the nose, often asking me “What’s up, Doc?” with these unwanted lines. You need not “wook wike a wascawwy wabbit” anymore, now that Botox can be used to treat wrinkle lines caused by muscular over-activity.

Bunny lines are caused by overactivity of the procerus, levator nasi alaequae, and compressor naris muscles, which can be easily softened by the use of a small dose of Botox injected on the upper sidewalls of the nose where these small subcutaneous muscles are located.

Get rid of Bunny Lines with Botox. (Athleo Louis Cambre, MD, Los Angeles Plastic Surgeon)

Botox for bunny lines

Botox And Dysport Can Improve Bunny Lines

It is possible to administer BOTOX in this area to reduce the appearance of bunny lines, and generally the area requires fewer units than other areas. (Jerome Edelstein, MD, Toronto Plastic Surgeon)

Botox and Bunny Lines

Botox works well in the treatment of “bunny lines”. Treatment may take 5 days to take effect in may last 3 to 4 months. (Tom J. Pousti, MD, FACS, San Diego Plastic Surgeon)

Can Botox Help with Bunny Lines?

Botox can definitely be used help lessen those little lines on your nose that occur with certain facial expressions. (Steven E. Rasmussen, MD, FAAD, Austin Dermatologist)

No bunnies for you!

“Bunny lines are cute on bunnies not on us.” As you can see Botox is one of the better approaches for treating bunny lines. You will see optimal improvements about two weeks after injections. You may need to have the procedure done more than once, so be prepared.

Botox Can Absolutely Improve Bunny Lines

Be sure to use a board certified dermatologist or facial plastic surgeon with much experience. (Yael Halaas, MD, Manhattan Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Botox works very well on bunny lines around the nose

Botox or dysport for bunny lines on the nose

Botox For Unwanted Bunny Lines

Botox can work to effectively treat unwanted bunny lines. Like other areas of the face, bunny lines come about due to excessive creasing secondary to the underlying muscle movement. Botox injected into the offending muscles can reliably stop the muscle from moving and create a much smoother, more relaxed appearance in this region. (John M. Hilinski, MD, San Diego Facial Plastic Surgeon)
