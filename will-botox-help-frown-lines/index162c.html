Will Botox Help Frown Lines?

Botox will most definitely help with 11s frown lines. A plastic surgeon should evaluate your muscle activity and determine how many units you require.

I typically administer 20 – 40 units for the glabella (this amount will vary). You will start noticing your 11s softening 2-3 days after your treatment.

Final results are noticeable 2 weeks post treatment. Your results will last 3 – 4 months and with each maintenance treatment your results will be more and more apparent. (Omar Fouda Neel, MD, FRCSC, FACS, Montreal Plastic Surgeon)

This is a resounding yes. Those lines are exactly the ones that botox treats. I would be very confident these lines will go with a botox treatment. Perhaps only 70-100% gone first time but almost fully gone within 2 or 3 treatments. (Paul Munsanje, MD, London Physician)

Botox and Frown lines

Botox will be useful in preventing the lines from deepening in the future.

In addition to the Botox, you may need to inject a filler to help with the volume loss due to the deeper crease. You will see a more significant improvement with the filler. You can consider Restylane Silk, Beletoro, or Juvederm.

These filler typically last 6 months+ whereas the Botox will typically last 3-4 months. It is important to note that if you chose only to do the filler without the Botox, the filler will not last as long because the filler will be broken down faster with the constant movement of the frown muscle. (John Tang, MD, Los Gatos Physician)

Can Botox Help Frown Lines

Botox ( like Xeomin and Dysport) is a medication injected into the target muscles of the face to selectively relax them for fewer/softer wrinkles. Vertical lines caused by depressor muscles in the glabella or 11s between the eyes can definitely be treated by Botox and other neuromodulator medications.

In fact, it was the first indication approved by the FDA. I do recommend a consultation for new patients. Does Botox Get Rid Of 11s

Botox can soften the 11 lines and if it does not ,then one can add filler used off-label to fill them as well. (Steven Wallach, MD, New York Plastic Surgeon)
Botox for 11s

Botox works by blocking signals from the nerves to the muscles. The injected muscle then can no longer contract, which causes the wrinkles to relax and soften. Yes, Botox can be used to treat the glabella (11’s) region.

You will start to see the effects of the Botox 1 week after injection and full effect in 2 weeks. You will need injections every 3-6 months as the Botox wears off. I recommend having a consultation with an expert injector in your area to determine the best treatment plan for you. (Hardik Soni, MD (not currently practicing), Summit Emergency Medicine Physician)

Botox for the “11’s” (Glabellar Furrows)

Botox works best for wrinkles caused by dynamic movement… i.e. movement of muscles causing the creases. As a result, it works well for lines between the eyebrows (so called “11’s” or glabellar furrows) as well as crow’s feet (periorbital rhytides).

In my opinion best option to get consistent and good results is to see a doctor who performs a large number of these types of procedures. . (Andrew Kaufman, MD, Los Angeles Dermatologic Surgeon)

11s are bothersome for many and a neuromodulator such as Botox, Dysport, or Xeomin can definitely help soften the lines which will provide you with a rejuvenated look for a period of 3-4 months. It is a very safe treatment, but you must ensure that you are being treated by an experienced injector who truly understands facial anatomy. (Anup Patel, MD, MBA, Orlando Physician)
