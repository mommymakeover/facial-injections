Botox minimizes motion. Restylane fills in crevices. If the prominent deep forehead or glabellar wrinkle is the main problem, Restylane or another filler will minimize or remove the wrinkle. In the crow’s feet area, Botox is usually preferred because it weakens the muscles that cause the crow’s feet when you smile or squint, as you do when taking photos for a wedding.

For the most complete improvement in the glabella (between the eyebrows), Restylane and Botox often provide the most complete result. (Richard Weiss, MD, Newport Beach Oculoplastic Surgeon)

Botox improves longevity of Juvederm and Restylane

The best example of the synergism of Botox and Juvederm or Restylane is the glabellar wrinkle (between the eyes). The filler fills the line,the Botox relaxes the muscle that pulls the eyebrows together. Without the Botox the filler will breakdown quickly because of the movement.

Botox and Restylane useful combo

Each is useful in different areas. Botox is best used for forehead and crow’s feet wrinkles.

Restylane is best for fuller lips and wrinkles around the mouth. (Daniel J. Ladd Jr., DO, Austin Dermatologist)

Botox and Restylane are complimentary products

Botox and Restylane compliment each other. Botox will help to soften existing wrinkles, helping prevent further deepening, and prevent the occurrence of new wrinkles by locally paralyzing the muscles that produce them. Fillers such as Restylane and Juvederm fill the wrinkle in.

Most clinicians use Botox to treat crow’s feet, frown lines between the brows and forehead wrinkles. Fillers are used to treat the ‘smile lines’, marionette lines–the lines that develop from the corner of the mouth downward to make us look like the marionette puppet, scars, and to volumize areas that become hollow and look like the area is not as full as it once was.

Using the combination of both Botox and Restylane can substantially reduce the signs of aging leaving a youthful appearance. (Armando Soto, MD, FACS, Orlando Plastic Surgeon)
