Botox Columbus Ohio: Cost And Before After Photos

Botox is the name of a drug produced by an American company. Since 1989, this tool has been used in neurology in the treatment of people suffering from muscle spasms. Nowadays Botox is a popular cosmetic procedure. What kind of procedure is it, how does it happen, what are the contraindications for its conduct – all this we will consider in this article.

What can and should be done after the injection?

Botox injections are often called “lunch break procedure”, so immediately after the procedure you can drive or go to work. However, that the result of injections does not disappoint you, within 4 hours after the procedure you need to be in an upright position and constantly strain your facial muscles within 24 hours.

How quickly becomes noticeable effect of the procedure?

The first result of the injection becomes noticeable after 5-7 days, when the muscles gradually begin to relax.

On average, the maximum therapeutic effect is shown on day 14-15. There are observations of a very fast action – already on 2-3 days, and delayed for 3-4 weeks.

In some cases, Botox is most effective?

With the help of botox, you can eliminate the vertical wrinkles between the eyebrows (this is considered the most effective), horizontal wrinkles on the forehead, lower the raised eyebrows, eliminate oblique wrinkles on the lateral surfaces of the bridge of the nose, on the back of the nose (wrinkles of anger) and “crow’s feet” .

But to smooth the wrinkles on the eyelids, smooth the nasolabial folds, eliminate the vertical wrinkles above and under the lips, smooth the vertical and horizontal wrinkles of the neck with the help of one botox is unreal.

This requires an integrated approach. In addition, in some cases, injections in the lower third of the face can lead to unpredictable consequences (possibly ptosis of soft tissues).

Are there any contraindications to injections?

Contraindications to the administration of Botox are myasthenia gravis (muscle weakness) and other disorders of neuromuscular transmission, hemophilia (blood clotting disorders), pregnancy, lactation, antibiotic-aminoglycoside, anticoagulants, antiplatelet agents, relanium and baclofen, inflammatory processes on the skin, any common diseases in the acute stage, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, renal or hepatic insufficiency.

It is undesirable to carry out the procedure in the first days of the menstrual cycle, as well as to people aged with a significant lowering of the soft tissues.

Are there people on which Botox does not work?

Insensitivity to the neurotoxin is very rare. It exists (according to different data) in about 0.1-2% of people.

At what age can Botox be injected?

BOTOX WORKS

In principle, Botox is permitted for use in cosmetology purposes at the age of 18 to 65 years. It is most reasonable to make injections from 30-33 years old, when mimic wrinkles begin to appear intensively due to a decrease in the elasticity of the skin. But if at an earlier age you constantly wrinkle, then with a preventive purpose it can be done earlier.

At the age of 60 years, Botox should be used very carefully, in any case it is necessary to begin rejuvenation with surgical lifting (neurotoxin helps to get rid of facial wrinkles, but not able to eliminate excess skin).

Could Botox Be Addictive?

Yes, but only in a positive way. If after the action of the drug after the first injections the effect persists for 4-6 months, after several years of application the effect persists for up to 10-12 months, and in some cases even longer. With the termination of Botox use, the toxin effect gradually weakens and muscle movements return to normal. However, after repeated injections, the habit of frowning and frowning, which was one of the reasons for the formation of wrinkles, gradually disappears.

What side effects are possible?

Possible complications are not associated with the drugs themselves, they can occur when the method and place of administration is incorrectly chosen, inadequate dose or if sterility is not observed.

Over time, they all disappear. Even less common are respiratory infections, flu syndrome and nausea.

In addition, as a result of excessive (unprofessional) administration of the drug a person can become “frozen” (like a mask), however, within 3-4 months these troubles disappear themselves.

Can creams “with the effect of botox” compare with the action of injections?

Such relaxing creams, which in the last two years have appeared in many selective, pharmacy, professional and even mass brands, can reduce the number of fine wrinkles, but the depth of penetration into the muscles of the face, creating, for example, wrinkles between the eyebrows, they can not be compared with injections.

How with the help of botox can get rid of hyperhidrosis (increased sweating)?

Neurotoxin blocks the transfer of momentum not only to the muscles, but also to the sweat glands, reducing sweating. After intradermal injections of botox, the ceasing of sweating occurs on day 2-3. The effect persists for 6-12 months. Thermoregulation, however, is not impaired, and the use of the drug even helps to improve the skin: it reaches a normal level of moisturizing. Since injections in the palms, axillary or feet are painful enough, an anesthetic cream is applied to the skin before injecting.

At what other diseases botox injections are used?

The main property of Botox is the ability to relax the muscle into which it is injected. Therefore, it injections are effective in those cases when it is necessary to get rid of muscle overstrain and spasms.

Among the diseases that are treated with Botox are some forms of headaches and migraines, various forms of dystonia, infantile cerebral palsy, strabismus, the consequences of stroke, craniocerebral trauma, multiple sclerosis and other diseases of the brain and spinal cord, painful muscle cramps in the neck, belt and back (including post-traumatic and sports), various tics, stammering, constipation, urination disorders and prostate adenoma. And more recently it has been discovered that Botox also accelerates the healing process of lesions and prevents the formation of scars.
