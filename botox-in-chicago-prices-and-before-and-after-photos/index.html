Botox In Chicago Prices And Before & After Photos

Botox in Chicago is a drug that contains inactivated botulinum toxin.

Injection of the Botox solution into various muscle groups on the face (in the forehead, eye area or nasolabial sulcus) leads to a permanent relaxation of the corresponding facial muscles, so that the mimic wrinkles in the injection area disappear or decrease.

Is it possible to increase the volume of lips with Botox?

This misconception arose because of the misuse of the names of various drugs. Botox on the face is used only to reduce facial wrinkles.

If you want to increase the volume of the lips, you need injections of intradermal fillers, such as Restylane or Juvederm.

Botox application features.

The constant reduction of facial muscles leads over time to the appearance of facial wrinkles. Such wrinkles cosmetologists call – dynamic.

BOTOX Treatment Is Able To Reduce The Activity Of The Designated Facial Muscles

The introduction of botulinum toxin into mimic muscles (causing wrinkles) causes a persistent long-term relaxation of these muscles, which leads to the disappearance or reduction of wrinkles in the area of injections.

Indications for use of Botox for cosmetic purposes in Chicago

  • wrinkles on the forehead, and between the eyebrows
  • wrinkles around the eyes – the so-called “crow’s feet”
  • pronounced nasolabial folds.

Botox injections take only 10-15 minutes. The maximum result after the procedure is 10-14 days. After applying the Botox, the effect lasts approximately 6 months, after which you can undergo a second procedure.

It should be noted that Botox is recommended by the FDA (the most prestigious medical organization in the US), as one of the best methods of removing facial wrinkles. It should be noted that with very deep wrinkles, Botox injections will allow them to only be reduced, but most likely they will not be completely removed.

Therefore, with deep wrinkles, Botox injections are carried out in combination with injections into the base of the wrinkles of intradermal fillers based on hyaluronic acid – “Restylane” or “Juvederm” preparations.

Botox Analogues

The analogues of Botox include, first of all, such a drug as “Dysport”, as well as preparations “Xeomin” and “Myobloc”. All these drugs are slightly different from each other, and therefore are not completely interchangeable. Consultation of a specialist and the following information will help you find out what is best for you – Dysport or Botox.

What is better than Botox or Dysport?

Dysport and Botox have very similar mechanisms of action (due to neuromuscular blockage of facial muscles with botulinum toxin).

Dysport contains less protein, and therefore has a lower protein load than Botox. This means that the introduction of Dysport will cause the formation by the immune system of fewer antibodies directed against it.

Our body uses antibodies to detect and destroy foreign proteins (antigens). A smaller number of antibodies means a longer duration of action. Therefore, the effect of Dysport should be somewhat longer than from Botox.

However, when injected into the soft tissues of the face, Dysport captures a larger volume of soft tissues during injection, spreading more than botox. This is both good and bad. And, so it means for the patient less chance of discomfort, swelling or hemorrhage after injections.

The bad thing is that the distribution of the disport in the tissues is less controlled, and if the doctor’s skills are insufficient, the drug can spread to undesirable areas (especially around the eyes), causing adverse side effects, for example, ptosis of eyebrows or fuzzy vision.

headache, bruising, pain at the injection site. If injections are carried out on the forehead, if the wrong dose of Botox is chosen, it is possible to lower the eyebrows and not be able to raise them (however, their natural position will return within a few months).

When injecting into the eye area, inexperienced doctors may allow the drug solution to spread excessively in soft tissues in the eye sockets, which can lead to fuzzy vision for a very long period of time.

You are more likely to get a hematoma at the injection site, if you are taking tablets containing aspirin or ibuprofen in the near future. These drugs dilute blood and increase bleeding. Since the last intake of these drugs should take 2 weeks, before you make botox injections.

For pregnant and lactating women, as well as for those who plan to become pregnant, the procedure is contraindicated. The same applies to people with neurological diseases.
