Movement after Botox

As with many aesthetic procedures there are a lot of myths that patients hear that simply are not medically feasible. Fast acting Botox is one of them. When you are injected with either Botox or Disport the medication travels to the end of a motor nerve and blocks the motion of a chemical responsible to stimulate another nerve going to a muscle which would normally then contract and move.

Botox and Disport prevent this stimulation and thus your muscle can’t move for about 3-4 months. The effects takes up to 14 days to really set. Most patients sense a change in the ability to move certain muscles in about 3 days ( in your case the muscles between your brows) and that gets even more profound at day 6 and by day 14 completely set.

So you are perfectly normal to not see any effect in a day. I would give it a good week and se the results I’m fairly sure you will be pleased. (Glenn Messina, MD, Commack Physician)
