In the final analysis, the important thing is the bill to pay after treatment. There are two ways to charge for Botox: by the unit, and by the area. Both are fair if applied consistently.

There is an implied guarantee in the area treatment method. If you still have movement after treatment with the area method, your doctor should re-inject you at no cost to you. My clients prefer the charge by the unit, when they have a preference.

In general, this method allows adjustment for individual preference in terms of movement of facial muscles after treatment. If a client wants absolute stillness, then adjustments upward in dose (and cost) are made. If some residual is desired, then adjustments downward in dose and cost are made.
