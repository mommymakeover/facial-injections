When Does Botox Start To Wear Off?

Botox will typically wear off around 3 months, sometimes a little more, sometimes less. (Satyen Undavia, MD, Philadelphia Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Botox effects take 3-4 months to completely resolve. There is not a way to speed up the process. (Matthew Elias, DO, FAAD, Fort Lauderdale Dermatologic Surgeon)

The effects from a Botox treatment usually will last a few months. There is no way to speed up this process. With more time, your appearance should return to how it was before your treatment. (Michael I. Echavez, MD, San Francisco Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Botox effects will wear off over the next 2-3 months to restore your previous facial appearance. (Nelson Castillo, MD, Atlanta Plastic Surgeon)

Botox is a purified protein used to address wrinkles associated with facial expression. Most people enjoy the results of their Botox treatment for 3-4 months. At that time the muscle are back to normal and a maintenance treatment is recommended. The maintenance treatment can be adjusted based on the results of the previous treatment. (Alex Eshaghian, MD, PhD, Encino Physician)

The Silky-smooth Results Of Botox

Botox effect can last up to three to four months. There is no way we can speed up this process. (Alain Michon, MD, Ottawa Physician)

There is no reversal agent for Botox. This is why you should make sure you have your injections done by an experienced physician. (Amjad Alkadri, MD, Merrillville Radiologist)

The effects will typically last 12-16 weeks so you will need to be patient while waiting for the effects to wear off. (Paul Cox, MD, Atlanta Family Physician)

While there is no way to remove Botox, it gradually wears off over the course of 3-4 months. (Inessa Fishman, MD, Atlanta Facial Plastic Surgeon)

The botox will wear off gradually usually in about 3-4 months. (Jean M. Casello, MD, Northborough Physician)

Botox generally lasts 3-4 months but will gradually wear off. You don’t need to get involved with any expensive therapy to shorten the time–not worth it. (Melvin Elson, MD, Nashville Dermatologist)
