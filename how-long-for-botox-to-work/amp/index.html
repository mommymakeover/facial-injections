How Long For Botox To Work?

Five to ten days

Typically, Botox cosmetic takes effect in 5-10 days and lasts between 2-4 months. This does vary widely as some individuals notice the effect within hours.

There does not seem to be a specific limit as to how often or how long one can receive Botox.

Some patients come in every few weeks and treat different areas on the face rotating the areas of treatment. (Janet Turkle, MD, Indianapolis Plastic Surgeon)

In some cases the same day you can see but usually 3 days will be the normal to see the botox results. (Jhonny Salomon, MD, Miami Plastic Surgeon)

Once injected, you can see results by the next day but final results take 10 days or so. Botox can last only 8 weeks in some to over 6 months in a few lucky patients. (Kurtis Martin, MD, Cincinnati Plastic Surgeon)

40 Year Old Woman Treated With Botox With Doctor Daniel Levy, MD, Bellevue Dermatologic Surgeon

Facial muscle relaxation following Botox injection usually begins 3-5 days following the injection. Full muscle paralysis typically takes 10-14 days following injection.

If you still experience movement after two weeks, it is best to see your provider for evaluation. (Steven J. Rottman, MD, FACS, Baltimore Plastic Surgeon)

It takes 4-10 days to see the full effect of Botox. You need it every 3- 4 months or so. Some patients don’t need it for 5-6 months but this is an exception. I have noticed a few ‘ non responders’ ( people who do not have any response to Botox).

This means that they have anti bodies to Botox and would require Xeomin ( the next generation toxin, which is pure and hence lower risk of being a non responder).

Botox Results Are Seen Within 2-3 Days of Injection

In the vast majority of patients the results of botox are seen within 2 to 3 days of injection. In rare cases, benefits may be seen as early as 24 hours following the procedure.

The response to botox becomes progressively stronger for approximately 5 to 7 days after treatment.

At this point, most patients have an excellent clinical result and are very satisfied with the results. Botox is often repeated at 3 to 4 months month intervals. These injections can be repeated indefinitely. Long-term problems aren’t associated with the use of botox.

When botox is used for prolonged periods the muscles can fatigue. This often results in a decreased botox requirement. (Richard J. Bruneteau, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)

Botox is slower than Dysport

Botox begins working within 3 to 5 days. A competitor, Dysport, begins working in 2 to 3 days.

Both products last about 3 to 4 months in most patients. You can undergo Botox as frequently as every 3 months-or as infrequently as you like.

Wrinkles can be static (visible at rest) or dynamic (visible only when squinting or frowning). The advantage of undergoing regular injections is that your static wrinkles will gradually get better and better. If you space out your injections too much, your muscles will have a chance to fully recover between treatments, and you will not tend to see the same degree of improvement in your static wrinkles.

In my practice I have seen Botox last as long as 9 months however, it most commonly lasts for 4 months. There is no limit to how long one can keep getting Botox treatments. (George C. Peck, Jr, MD, West Orange Plastic Surgeon)

Botox: Onset and Duration of Effect

Botox onset (i.e., when the effects become noticeable) is generally between 3 and 6 days. This varies by dose (number of units), muscle group (e.g., forehead vs. frown lines), and patient.

The treatment will wear off and the nerves endings generate new connections to the injected muscles.

This may be as soon as 3 months or as long as 6, again, depending on the above factors. (Evan Ransom, MD, FACS, Bay Area Facial Plastic Surgeon)
