Botox Around Eyes Before And After

Botox usage for Crow’s feet around the eyes

Botox is an excellent treatment for the dynamic wrinkles at the sides of the eyes called Crow’s feet. Most people receive treatment every 3-4 months for this area with nice, safe & natural results.

It can also be used for the forehead and around the mouth for smoker’s lines. Make sure to have an expert injector such as a dermatologist treating you to optimize results & safety. (Benjamin Barankin, MD, FRCPC, Toronto Dermatologic Surgeon)

Botox works great for the crow’s feet. It is also great for the 11s (the area between your eyes), the forehead wrinkles, dimpling chins, neck bands, lines on the upper nose called bunny lines, etc.

Botox Treatment Is A Simple 10-minute Procedure

The effects will start in about 3 days and reach maximum effect in about 7-10 days. The results will last for around 3 months. I’d recommend you consult with one of core aesthetic injectors (facial plastic surgeon, plastic surgeon, oculoplastic surgeon, or dermatologist). (William Marshall Guy, MD, The Woodlands Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Botox is great for Crowsfeet around the eyes

Botox is usually the preferred treatment for crowsfeet. However it is important to understand how Botox work and what it will and will not do. Botox temporarily relaxes muscles and helps dynamic lines (those that form with facial expression).

So when you smile after a Botox crowsfeet treatment, the area does not wrinkle up with expression. You may notice that you have some fine lines in your skin even when you are not making an expression (static lines).

These wrinkles in the skin are not generally improved by Botox….at least in the short run. However, the dynamic lines are caused by the repeated folding of the skin with expression. If you keep up with your Botox, as the skin regenerates (as it naturally does) the static lines will not form.

So Botox helps dynamic lines. However it will also often help static lines over time. (Marc Cohen, MD, Philadelphia Oculoplastic Surgeon)
