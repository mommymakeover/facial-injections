Preparations botulinum toxin (mainly botox) in recent years have become popular as a means for smoothing facial wrinkles, that is wrinkles caused by excessive activity of the subcutaneous musculature. For example, in 2006, the United States produced 11.5 million Botox injections. Advertising claims that smoothing wrinkles with this poison is a quick, safe procedure with a low risk of side effects. Sometimes the authors of advertising articles go so far as to represent botox as a kind of non-toxic form of botulinum toxin, which has nothing in common with a deadly poison.

However, more recently, there have been reports of frequent cases of serious side effects and fatalities associated with the use of this drug. So, botox is dangerous and what do we know about it?

BOTOX Cosmetic Can Help You Look Younger

Tamed Poison

In the 70s of the last century, American ophthalmologist Alan Scott began to test an unusual medicine prepared on the basis of botulinum toxin, the most powerful natural poison that causes the lethal form of food poisoning – botulism, on his patients suffering from blepharospasm (involuntary eye-shutter).

The symptoms of botulism were first described in the 19th century. The main feature of this disease is progressive paralysis, which in most cases leads to death as a result of stopping breathing.

Since in the past poisoning often occurred with the consumption of sausages infected with a bacterium that produces this toxin, it was called botulinum toxin, that is, sausage poison (botulus in Latin means “sausage”), and poisoning itself – botulism.

Although botulinum toxin is certainly dangerous, Scott’s ideas were not unexpected, since even in the 1950s, scientists established that a purified and heavily diluted toxin can be used, albeit with great caution, for medical purposes to relax muscle spasms. Soon, seeing a high percentage of recovery, Scott’s example was followed by other doctors, gradually expanding the area of application of the toxin.

BOTOX Injections Are Safe And Effective

In particular, botulinum toxin was used to treat strabismus and hemispasm (spasm of one half of the face). Even then, some doctors who treated patients with botulinum toxin noticed an interesting side effect. In the areas of injections, a wonderful disappearance of wrinkles occurred on the face, for example, interbrow folds on the forehead or folds in the corners of the mouth. The patient, thus, not only got rid of the pesky spasm, but also found a young, relaxed and affable expression. Soon, a delicate stream of unusual patients-wealthy ladies aged 50 and over who were ready to take risks to everyone at least for a while to be younger-stretched to the doctors of neurologists.

There are several types of botulinum toxin, which differ in immunological and chemical properties. The first commercial preparation containing botulinum toxin was Botox.

Manufacturers chose botulinum toxin type A (all types of toxins are denoted in Latin letters), which for a long time remained the only type of botulinum toxin used in medicine.

In 1989, the FDA (American Food and Drug Administration) officially approved the use of botox to treat a number of diseases associated with involuntary muscle contractions. And although wrinkles in the list of indications for the use of this drug have not yet appeared, more and more doctors began to use this medicine, as they say in America, “off label”, that is, not for its intended purpose.

Only in 2002 the FDA finally approved the use of Botox Cosmetic for cosmetic purposes – to eliminate mimic wrinkles on the forehead and around the eyes.

From this moment, the triumphal ascent of Botox began to the top of success. It got to the point that in the US this procedure has become so popular that it is offered almost at every step – in beauty parlors and even in sports clubs. In Europe and Russia, along with botox, a disport drug (Dysport) is used.

Such different wrinkles

Let’s talk now about where the wrinkles come from and why they disappear after injections of botulinum toxin.

The wrinkles that so afflict women, not only look different, but have different causes of occurrence. True age wrinkles arise due to changes occurring in the skin itself. All that you have heard about the destruction of collagen and the accumulation of damages under the influence of UV radiation and other harmful factors, refers to this type of wrinkles. But there are other wrinkles, which are just folds of the skin, formed on the site of the usual spasm of the subcutaneous muscles.

With contraction, the muscle shortens and wrinkles the skin, and when relaxed, it returns to its original position and “pulls” it back. In young women, the skin is elastic, it reacts quickly to such movments of muscles and is restored, but with age such exercises are given to it already harder.

Such wrinkles are usually formed where our facial expression is most active – around the eyes, on the bridge of the nose, on the forehead. Therefore they are called mimic wrinkles. If you make the muscle completely relax, then wrinkle as if by magic is smoothed.
