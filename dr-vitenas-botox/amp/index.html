Deep Tissue Massage Not a Threat to Botox Results
May 23, 2019
Thank you for your question and congratulations on your recent Botox session. After about four hours, the Botox will have sufficiently absorbed into the targeted muscles, meaning that migration is not an issue. Having your deep neck massage four days after your injections should not have any effect on your Botox results. If you have any further concerns or notice anything unusual, contact your Botox provider for his/her advice.  
Got Botox injections with makeup still on injection site. Should I be concerned?
Cleaning the Botox Injection Site
May 23, 2019
Thank you for your question. Although it is not necessary to remove all of your makeup before your Botox injections, the site should be cleaned prior administration. However, the chance of this causing any problems or infection is very small. Do not worry, but If you have any problems such as unusual redness or discomfort, contact your provider. You may want to bring this up at your next Botox session, especially if you notice your dermatologist is again not prepping the injection site.
Can I receive a Botox touch up before the recommended two weeks, if so what are the risks?
Botox Touch-Up Before Two Weeks
May 23, 2019
Thank you very much for your question. It sounds like your new Botox provider may have been using a conservative approach during the first appointment. As it takes one to two weeks for the results from your Botox procedure to become apparent, the best advice is to wait the full two weeks before getting a touch-up. This will give your provider a chance to assess the final results and apply the right amount during your touchup to reach your aesthetic goals.
